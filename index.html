ERNIE
Enhanced Research Network
Informatics Environment

View Project on GitHub

Deep collaboration networks underlie scientific innovations that impact society and individual health. ERNIE (Enhanced Research Network Informatics Environment) is a data platform with workflows and models that enables the discovery and analysis of these networks in scientific research. ERNIE derives from a thought experiment (Williams et al. Cell 163:21-23, 2015) that we built upon and has since grown into a knowledge resource for research assessment that (i) aggregates diverse data for discovery (ii) enables multidimensional measurements of research achievements. ERNIE is designed and developed by NET ESolutions Corporation (NETE). The project has been funded in part as a Fast Track Small Business Innovative Research award with Federal funds from the National Institute on Drug Abuse, National Institutes of Health, US Department of Health and Human Services. Phase I was completedin Feb, 2018. Phase II commenced on Sept 30, 2018. Phase II is focused on building a user community and 'productionizing' the platform.

Emphasis is placed on the use of Open Source technologies. The data in ERNIE are drawn in through custom ETL processes from both publicly available and commercial sources. Initial server infrastructure has been set up, core data have been scraped, leased, parsed, loaded, and partially curated and beta-user studies are under way. The project team will add additional data sources as and when they become available. Both the infrastructure and data model are continuously upgraded as we add new data sources and prune out less useful ones.

In its first phase of development, ERNIE was tested in case studies spanning drug development, medical devices/diagnostics, behavioral interventions, and solutions for drug discovery. These case studies focused on substance abuse and relied on our core workflow consisting of data mining, linking, and network analysis.

We recently formalized a partnering agreement with Elsevier and by July 1, 2019 we will have completed transition from Web of Science to Scopus as the bibliographic backbone of ERNIE. Similarly for patents, we will be using IPDD from Lexis-Nexis instead of the Derwent Patent Citation Index, which we were previously using.

The project philosophy is an amalgam of academic-style research and industry-style development by a multidisciplinary team embracing agile practices. Data Engineers, the frontline employees in our group, are typically just out of a Master's degree and are hired on one year contracts. They are expected to become productive within 60 days of joining the team, contribute substantially for the remaining 10 months, and eventually progress to an external position with substantially increased material benefits (below).
