ERNIE (Enhanced Research Network Informatics Environment) originates from (i) a thought experiment (inspired by Williams et al. Cell 163:21-23, 2015) intended to address the research assessment problem (ii) our prior experience in building a database of linked data for internal use by a federal agency. These interests grew into an effort to create ERNIE as a knowledge resource for research assessment that would aggregate diverse data for discover and enable multidimensional measurements of research achivements while being accesible to the broader community. ERNIE is designed and developed by NET ESolutions Corporation (NETE). The project has been funded in part as a Fast Track Small Business Innovative Research award with Federal funds from the National Institute on Drug Abuse, National Institutes of Health, US Department of Health and Human Services. In Feb 2018, Phase I was completed. Phase II has since been awarded and commenced on Sept30, 2018. Phase II is focused on building a user community and 'productionizing' the platform. We gratefully acknowledge constructive critique and advice from the Program staff at NIDA.

We are not involved with this other project also named ERNIE, which contains source code for a language representation model. However, we have exchanged cordial communications with the thunlp/ERNIE folks and wished them the same success that we aspire to.

ERNIE is a data platform, with analytical workflows, that enables the discovery and analysis of collaboration networks in scientific research. In its first phase of development, ERNIE was tested using case studies spanning drug development, medical devices/diagnostics, behavioral interventions, and solutions for drug discovery. These case studies, focused on substance abuse, relied on our core workflow consisting of data mining, linking, and network analysis.

Emphasis is placed on the use of Open Source technologies. At present, ERNIE resides in a PostgreSQL 11 database in a Centos 7.4 VM in the Microsoft Azure cloud. The data in ERNIE are drawn in through custom ETL processes from both publicly available and commercial sources. Additional servers provide Solr and Neo4J support and a Spark cluster is provisioned as needed. Initial server infrastructure has been set up, core data have been scraped, leased, parsed, loaded, and partially curated and beta-user studies are under way. The project team will add additional data sources as and when they become available. The infrastructure and data model is continuously upgraded as we add new data sources and prune out less useful ones.

The project philosophy is an amalgam of academic-style research and industrial efficiency by a multidisciplinary team embracing agile practices. Data Engineers, the frontline employees in our group, are typically just out of a Master's degree and are hired on one year contracts. They are expected to become productive within 60 days of joining the team, contribute substantially for the remaining 10 months, and eventually progress to an extermal position with substantially increased material benefits (below). Recently, we formalized a partnering agreement with Elsevier. As of July 1, 2019 we will have completed transition from Web of Science to Scopus as the bibliographic backbone of ERNIE. Similarly for patents, we will be using using IPDD from Lexis-Nexis instead of the Derwent Patent Citation Index, which we were previously using
